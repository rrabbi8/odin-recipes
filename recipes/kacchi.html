Kacchi Biryani

Ingredients

  • 1 kg mutton, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups basmati rice, washed and soaked in water for 30 minutes
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 cup ghee
  • 4-5 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 2-3 cinnamon sticks
  • 4-5 cardamom pods
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • A pinch of saffron
  • Fresh coriander leaves and mint leaves, chopped for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, marinate the mutton with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala powder, and salt. Mix well and set aside for at least 2 hours.
  2. In a large pot, heat ghee over medium heat. Add the onions and fry until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add the marinated mutton and cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the meat is browned.
  4. Add 1-2 cups of water, cover the pot and let it cook on low heat for 30-40 minutes, until the mutton is tender.
  5. In a separate pot, boil 8-10 cups of water. Add the cinnamon sticks, cardamom pods, cloves, and bay leaf to the boiling water.
  6. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the boiling water. Cook the rice until it is about 70% done. Drain the water and set the rice aside.
  7. Preheat the oven to 180Â°C.
  8. In a large oven-safe pot or pan, layer the cooked mutton and green chilies at the bottom.
  9. Add the partially cooked rice on top of the mutton, spreading it out evenly.
  10. Mix the warm milk and saffron in a bowl and pour it over the rice.
  11. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or aluminum foil, and place it in the oven for 30-40 minutes.
  12. Remove the pot from the oven, and let it sit for 5-10 minutes.
  13. Garnish with fresh coriander and mint leaves before serving.

Your delicious Kacchi Biryani is ready!
